How many ways to spell 'Guns N Roses'?

Over  at last.fm, they are making a big push to clean up the world of music metadata.  A substantial fraction of the ID3 tags embedded in MP3 files are missing, inconsistent, or flat-out wrong.  last.fm has released a fingerprinter that they can use to resolve any track to a unique ID based upon the audio content of the track.   RJ, one of the founders of last.fm, indicates that in the end they may actually be able to find out how many ways there are to spell "Guns N’ Roses – Knockin’ on Heaven’s Door".  Just to give you an idea of how much fun this can be, here are the top 12 ways to misspell 'Guns N' Roses', according to MusicBrainz. 

Update: Check out the comments section, RJ has posted over 400 variants of GNR collected by the lastfm fingerprinter so far.

Posted by Zac on September 11, 2007 at 12:09 PM EDT #

I think you're missing the point, Zac.

Suppose, for example, that you want to compile statistics by sampling what folks play in an application like Winamp--perhaps using that data to divvy up royalties for artists or composers. In that case, you're not so much interested in what is the "correct" way to spell an artist's name (assuming the artist can stick to one spelling in the first place!) as in all the ways that users of Winamp can misspell the artist's name. In that case, a handcrafted, "correct" spelling of the artist's name as determined by an editor is completely beside the point!

For that matter, suppose we want Winamp to link to the appropriate artist's page at AMG automagically while playing their songs. If you only match a handful of acceptable variants as determined by human editors, you'll miss lots and lots of chances to drive traffic to AMG's site...

Posted by Rob Szarka on September 16, 2007 at 04:34 PM EDT #

I agree wholeheartedly. As an amateur meddler in web APIs and trying to find and aggregate music myself, the non-standardization of tag data out there is infuriating.

In the end though, you want all of these different "interpretations" to funnel down to a single reference point (as opposed to having 120 different Guns 'N' Roses pages/entries/destinations.

Some kind of a one-to-many mapping of all possible G'n'Rs to a single unique entity would be very valuable. Fuzzy text matching can only get you so far.
